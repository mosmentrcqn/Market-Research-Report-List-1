<p><p>Pharmaceutical grade anion exchange resins are categorized into two main types: alkaline and acidic. Alkaline anion exchange resins effectively capture negatively charged molecules in basic environments, aiding in the purification processes of pharmaceuticals. Conversely, acidic anion exchange resins are designed to operate in acidic conditions, facilitating the removal of anions from solutions. Both types play crucial roles in drug formulation, ensuring the purity, stability, and efficacy of pharmaceutical products by selectively targeting and removing unwanted anionic contaminants.</p></p>
